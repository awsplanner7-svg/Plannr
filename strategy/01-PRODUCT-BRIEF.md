# Plannr — Product Brief

This document is the **source of truth** for App Store copy, social bios, pitch decks, investor convos, landing pages, and anything else where Plannr needs to be described. Keep it tight. Update it when the product changes.

---

## What Plannr Is

Plannr is a mobile app that lets a group plan a trip, event, or shared experience together — without descending into 800-message group chats and abandoned Notes docs.

Anyone in the group can drop in ideas. Everyone can vote on what they like. The organizer turns the winners into a checklist and a day-by-day itinerary. On the trip itself, the whole plan — including reservations, codes, and links — lives in one place that everyone can access.

It's built for the moments where one person ends up doing all the planning anyway, but wants the rest of the group to actually participate.

> Group trips fall apart in the group chat. Plannr is the shared space where everyone can dump ideas, vote on the good ones, and build a real itinerary together. The Type-A friend gets to plan without herding cats, and everyone else gets to participate without doing the work.

## Who It's For

**Primary user — The Type-A Organizer**
- Late 20s to mid-30s
- The friend in every group who ends up planning by default
- Usually women, but every group has one regardless
- Already opens Google Docs, Notes, and group chats to coordinate
- Tired of being the only one driving
- Examples in your life: you, your sister, your sister-in-law

**Secondary user — The Passive Participant**
- Same age range
- Wants to contribute opinions ("that day looks too packed") but won't do the work
- Won't download a "planning app" on their own
- Will download Plannr because the organizer sent them an invite link
- Examples in your life: your gf scanning a day to flag overscheduling

**Where this group lives**
- Bachelor / bachelorette parties
- Group ski trips, beach trips, vacation rentals
- Family vacations and reunions
- Honeymoons being planned by Type-A halves
- Group apartment moves, baby showers, big birthdays
- Wedding logistics among bridal parties

## Why It Exists (Origin)

The idea started when Anthony and his girlfriend moved into a new apartment. Her family kept sending suggestions of things to do and see — restaurants, decor stores, hikes. Then her sister visited and added more. The list became unmanageable. He wanted a clean place for people to suggest things he could like or veto.

While building, he realized this same problem hits him constantly with friends: he's 28, in the bachelor-party years, and every group he's in plans trips through chaotic group chats and dying Notes docs. There was an app for that (Batch), but its monetization (selling local excursions as ads) compromised the experience, and its itinerary and checklist features were thin. Plannr's bet: get the planning experience right, monetize quietly through referral links on the bookings users are already making.

The first real-world test of Plannr will be **the August proposal trip**, where Anthony's mom + sister and his girlfriend's mom + two sisters will use it together.

## Core Features (What Plannr Does Today)

| Feature | What it does | Status |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **Boards** | One container per trip/event. Group joins via invite code. | Built |
| **Suggestions + voting** | Anyone in the board drops ideas; everyone votes. | Built |
| **Checklist** | Approved suggestions become a checklist with assignees, links, descriptions. | Built |
| **Itinerary** | Day-by-day schedule with ordered items, times, locations. | Built |
| **Invite codes** | Shareable codes that let anyone join a board. | Built |
| **Leave / remove / rotate** | Members can leave; creators can remove members or rotate the invite code. | Backend done, UI pending |
| **Activity feed** | Real append-only log of who did what. | Planned (post-launch) |
| **Comments on suggestions** | React to ideas with text, not just votes. | Planned (post-launch) |
| **Public boards** | Discover what other groups did on similar trips. | Future moat |
| **Cost tracking** | Split-wise style for shared expenses. | Future |

## How Plannr Is Different

| | Plannr | Group chat + Notes doc | Wanderlog / TripIt | Batch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Group voting on ideas | Yes | No | No | Partial |
| Real itinerary with order | Yes | No | Yes | No |
| Real checklist with links | Yes | No | No | No |
| Built for groups (not solo) | Yes | Yes | No | Yes |
| Monetized via ads | No | n/a | No | Yes |
| Public boards (future) | Planned | No | No | No |

**The differentiator in one sentence:** Plannr is the only group trip app that handles *both* the messy idea-gathering phase *and* the day-of-trip "what's the plan" phase, without ads.

---

## What Plannr Is NOT

- Not a booking platform (we link out to the user's tool of choice)
- Not a chat app (comments yes, real-time chat no)
- Not a solo travel planner
- Not a guidebook / discovery app
- Not a payment app or expense splitter (yet)
- Not an ads-supported app

## Brand Voice

Plannr's personality is the **funny, slightly exhausted friend who's seen every group chat disaster** and has thoughts about it.

- Direct, dry, knowing
- Loves group trips, mocks the chaos
- Speaks like a person, not a product
- Specific over generic — refers to "the friend who only chimes in to veto," not "users with feedback"
- Faceless brand voice; never says "we" or "our team"
- The product itself has a voice — first-person captions work ("i watched another group chat plan a trip in 800 messages and i'm tired")

**Reference brands for voice:**
- Duolingo (chaotic mascot energy)
- Liquid Death (committed bit)
- Scrub Daddy (faceless but personality-rich)
- Wendy's Twitter (dry observation)

## Brand Vocabulary

**Use:**
- **Board** (not "trip" — Plannr is also good for non-trips)
- **Suggestion** (not "item" or "idea")
- **The planner** (not "admin" or "owner")
- **The group** (not "users")
- **The chaos** (the problem)
- **One plan, one place** (the promise)

**Avoid:**
- "Productivity" anything
- "Workflow" / "workspace"
- "Travel tech"
- "Solution" / "platform"
- Anything that sounds like a SaaS landing page
