## Universal Don'ts

- "Revolutionary" / "Disrupting" / "Reimagining"
- "AI-powered" (even if it eventually is — that's not the pitch)
- "Travel tech startup" (sounds like LinkedIn)
- Mention founder / "built by"
- All caps anything
- More than one emoji per bio

## Profile Picture Strategy

Until your real app icon exists:

**Option 1 — placeholder logo:** A bold sans-serif "P" on a solid colored background. Pick a color (suggestion: warm coral or muted teal — travel-adjacent, not the standard blue every productivity app uses). Square crop.

**Option 2 — typographic wordmark:** Just "plannr" in a single bold typeface on a solid background. Works at thumbnail size.

**Option 3 — pictogram:** A simple line illustration — a paper airplane, a checkmark, a pin. Avoid anything cute. Avoid anything generic-travel-y like a globe or suitcase.

After you have your real icon (see `06-APP-ICON-BRIEF.md`), replace these everywhere consistently.

---

## Banner / Header Image Strategy

Most platforms let you set a header. Don't stress about this v1. Suggestions:

- Solid color matching your icon background
- A blurred screenshot of the app
- A typographic banner: "the group trip app for the friend who always plans"

Avoid:
- Stock photos of mountains, beaches, friend groups
- Photos of physical maps, paper planners
- Anything that looks like every other travel app

---

## Linking Strategy

Everywhere you can put a link, link to **joinplannr.app**.

Until you have a real landing page (week 2-3), point joinplannr.app to either:
- A simple Carrd.co page (cheapest)
- A public Notion page (free)
- An App Store badge that just says "coming soon"

This is fine and even helps — the "coming soon" / waitlist vibe builds anticipation if you start posting content while the app isn't live yet.
